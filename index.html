Preface

Hello everyone, and welcome to my blog! Today, I want to embark on a different kind of project. It's easy to throw things away without a second thought, but what if we paused before tossing something in the trash? I'm starting an experiment where I'll be tracking the items I'd normally discard, and taking a moment to reflect on their role in my life.

Why am I doing this? Honestly, I've become increasingly aware of my trash footprint. It's time to examine my habits and see if there are ways to reduce waste. Can I reuse something in a creative way? Is it recyclable? Sometimes, I might even realize an item still has value and deserves to stay in my possession!, In addition after reading many different passionate authors and thier views about trash I also have gotten more concious about my own decisions to help with trash footprint. If you want to read more about these authors and their contributions and how they inspired me feel free to check out my expanded preface where I go into detail their work and how it has inspired me.

Overview

This week I am actually traveling to a musical instrument recycling/repair shop where I will be recycling them. Due to this, I decided that I was going to recycle all my instruments that are currently not producing the desired sound. I had a lot of emotional difficulty recycling my first-ever instrument. I connected to this instrument on a deeper level and had a lot of nostalgia and memories with it, so it was very difficult to let go of this instrument.

Categories

Instruments

A Tabla Old (playing percussion instrument) :
I got this in 8th grade when I started learning Tabla from a teacher outside of school, and ever since then I have put about 2000 hours into learning tabla and I am kind of a semi-professional musician right now for this specific instrument. And this has really helped me but currently this instrument really has no use as you see there is a tear in the instrument. I am currently recycling this instrument to a repair shop because it's not helpful for me as it doesn’t produce a desirable sound, however this is my first tabla and I will always be thankful.

A Piano :
Recently 2 years ago I found out that I have a very very good relative pitch. It's not perfect pitch like Charlie Puth but I have relatively very good pitch recognition, hence I started self learning piano. However we had to recently move and whilst moving something must’ve happened to this digital piano and now it no longer works, and produces many noises and I have never gotten a chance to trash it but this week it so happened that I am going to a music recycling center where I can give these items for the person to fix it and recycle it instead of just throwing it away in the trash.

A Maadal :
Again this instrument has also been with us for about 6 years and I have accompanied many of south asian folk singers that sing folk songs which this instrument is used for and I have used it so much that again the sound that this instrument produces isn’t desirable anymore and taking upon the opportunity to I am recycling all of these instruments which are much better if they are recycled and then the seller can resell them for other people to use and make proper use of these rather than it just staying at my house doing nothing hence I have decided to recycle this instrument as well.

Digital Items

A Desktop :
I have had this desktop for the past 8 years and my dad has used it for his own usage however recently he has gotten a newer and nicer laptop and hasn’t touched this desktop so we decided to recycle this in the electronics disposable center.

A Laptop :
In 10th grade high school I bought a gaming laptop where I have played many video games and I have put quite a lot of hours playing minecraft, fortnite and those games and currently after many many hours of usage and it also dropped while moving and currently the screen is cracked and doesn’t work anymore. I still never found a chance to recycle or trash this however doing this blog I realized and remembered that I have to recycle this laptop. I am still kind of sad to let this go as in this laptop I have made many memories playing games, doing projects and homeworks and everything so it's very sad to see it go away.

Some extra unused wires :
These are many wires that we accumulated as time passes throughout and I have been keeping all the extra unused wires that I am not going to need anymore however I never got a chance to recycle them as I had never had time to go to a recycle center for electronics however now that I have the chance today I will be recycling them today.

Cosmetic items

A antiperspirant cream :
I have a bit of hyperhidrosis issue and it kind of affects my palm because I sweat it a lot hence I am using this cream to help me deal with this, however it was over about 3 months ago and has been collecting dust so I decided to trash this item.

A perfume :
Similar to the antiperspirant cream, this has also been staying in my bathroom collecting dust as it was over already 3 weeks ago and I decided to trash them and document it today because I have a chance to do it.

A powder :
I recently bought this powder and I realized that it causes some rashes and I might be allergic to the contents of this powder hence I decided to trash it.

Item of the Week

I think the tabla is definitely the item of the week. I am a semi-professional tabla player, and I have spent more than 8 years playing and practicing this instrument so throwing away the first tabla that started it all was very sad and was a big step for me. I think that I have spent more than 400 hours at least on this tabla just practicing and around 8 hours or so performing these instruments in concerts. One fun moment was when I was performing on a stage one day and mid-performance this instrument just randomly goes out of tune making me change the pitch of it mid-performance and play and tune the instrument at the same time. It was a great and a really funny experience.

Coda

This was a great reflection as I learned to value all these things even more. I would try to reduce the waste as much as possible because I can reflect that everything that you own has some sort of importance to you so throwing them away feels a bit emotional. In addition due to reflections from last week I was able to be more conscious and recycle a lot of my items. Another thing I noticed is that I started remembering trash that I need to get rid of just because of this project as well, hence this made me trash a lot of items that have been doing nothing but collecting dust at my house.
